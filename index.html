Mathematics Assignment Questions

Instructions :-

  • Pick 1 question from Section A and 1 question from Section B.
  • Create a 15-page project for each question.

Section A

1. Using a graph demonstrate a function which is 1-1 but not onto.

2. Using a graph, draw a function which is invertible.

3. Construct a composition table using a binary operation (modulo). Addition, multiplication and verify the existence of the proper binary composition.

4. Draw the graph of y = sin-1x and using the graph of sin x, demonstrate the concept of mirror line about y = x and find its domain and range. Also the principal value.

5. Explore the principal value of the function sin-1x or any other inverse trigonometric function using a unit circle.

6. Find the derivative of a determinant of the order 3×3 and verify the same by other method.

7. Verify the consistency of the system of 3 linear equations (e.g., of 2 variables) and verify the same graphically. Give its geometric interpretation.

8. For a dependent system (non-homogeneous) of 3 linear equations of 3 variables, identify the number of solutions.

9. For a given function, give the geometric interpretation of the mean value theorem. Explain the significance of closed and open interval for continuity and differentiability property of theorems.

10. Explain the aspect of increasing and decreasing function using the geometric significance of dy/dx. Illustrate with proper example.

11. Explain the geometrical significance of point of inflection with example and illustrate it using graphs.

12. Explain and illustrate the concept of local maxima and local minima using graph.

13. Explain and illustrate the concept of absolute maxima and absolute minima using graph.

14. Elucidate the concept of definite integral representing a sum (i.e., limit of sum) and verify it by actual integration.

15. Demonstrate application of differential to solve the given problem.

16. Explain the conditional probability, the theorem of total probability & concepts of Bayes' theorem with suitable examples.

17. Explain types of Bivariate distribution & derive mean & variants of binomial probability distribution for a given function.

Section B

1. Using vector algebra, find the area of parallelogram and triangle and derive area analytically and verify the same.

2. Using vector algebra, prove the formulas of properties of triangle (Δ).

3. Using vector algebra, prove the formula of compound angle: sin(A ± B).

4. Describe the geometrical interpretation of scalar triple product for a given data and find the scalar triple product.

5. Find the image of a line with respect to a given plane.

6. Find the distance of a point from a given plane measured parallel to a given line.

7. Find the distance of a point from a line measured parallel to a given plane.

8. Find the area bounded by the parabola in an oblique line.

9. Find the area bounded by a circle and an oblique line.

10. Find the area bounded by an ellipse and an oblique line.

11. Find the area bounded by a circle and a chord.

12. Find the area bounded by two parabolas.

13. Find the area bounded by a circle and a parabola.
